Executive Summary

The St. James Parish Welcome Center is an "economic jewel" of the region, housing the local economic development office and serving as a hub for public events. The facility features a unique Cajun village design signature utilizing century-old historical structures moved to the site a decade ago.

This project is required to desperately expand and repair the aging infrastructure, including critical upgrades to parking, lighting, and structural modifications, revitalizing the center to meet strategic goals for the next decade.

Project Drivers & Benefits

Economic Development

Modernizes the primary tourism and welcome hub for the parish. A well-maintained facility is crucial for attracting business, generating employment, and bolstering the quality of life for residents.

Cultural Preservation

Restores century-old historical structures that are in desperate need of repair. The renovation respects the prior Cajun village design signature while integrating modern amenities.

Infrastructure & Safety

Addresses urgent needs in parking and lighting to accommodate high public utilization (approx. 7,065 sq. ft. of active space including museum and event areas).
