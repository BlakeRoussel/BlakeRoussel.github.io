Executive Summary

This project requests reauthorization of Capital Outlay funds to address the critical maintenance of St. James Parish's 75.4-mile road network. Following comprehensive assessments in 2022, this phase targets the mill, patch, and overlay of approximately 5 miles of the most severely damaged roadways. This initiative is vital to catching up on maintenance backlogs caused by previous contractor defaults, the COVID-19 pandemic, and Hurricane Ida.

Project Drivers & Benefits

Critical Infrastructure Recovery

Addresses the "severely damaged" tier of the parish road network. Without this intervention, the current maintenance cycle is projected to take over 20 years to complete.

Socioeconomic Impact

Transportation infrastructure is identified as having a substantial impact on all socioeconomic activities in the region, supporting local commerce and resident mobility.

Strategic Stabilization

Stabilizes the maintenance program after years of external disruptions (2018 Surety execution, 2020-21 Pandemic/Hurricanes), ensuring a return to a consistent rehabilitation cycle.
